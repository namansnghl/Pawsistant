F1 Student Resources

Important information and resources for international students on F1 visas.

F1 Visa Process

The F1 visa is a non-immigrant visa that allows foreign students to pursue education in the United States. The application process involves several steps including:

  • Obtaining an I-20 form from your school
  • Paying the SEVIS fee
  • Completing the DS-160 form
  • Scheduling and attending a visa interview

Maintaining F1 Status

To maintain your F1 status, you must:

  • Maintain a full course load (typically 12 credit hours for undergraduates)
  • Make normal progress toward completing your program
  • Maintain a valid I-20
  • Not work off-campus without authorization
  • Report any address changes to your DSO within 10 days

OPT and CPT

Optional Practical Training (OPT) and Curricular Practical Training (CPT) are employment authorizations for F1 students:

  • CPT: Work experience that is an integral part of your curriculum
  • OPT: Temporary employment directly related to your major area of study
  • Post-completion OPT: Up to 12 months of OPT after completing your degree
  • STEM Extension: Additional 24 months for eligible STEM degree holders

Travel Information

When traveling outside the U.S. as an F1 student, you need:

  • A valid passport (valid for at least 6 months in the future)
  • A valid F1 visa
  • A current I-20 signed for travel by your DSO within the last year
  • Proof of financial support and enrollment
